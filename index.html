Welcome to AR – GLASSHOUSE

Keywords - Augmented Reality, Mathematical Modelling, IoT, Greenhouse

With the vast growth of population, increased production of agricultural products is necessary. Although the amount of land available for agriculture is limited, the demand for food products based on agriculture is expanding. The Organic food supply is scarce in the current food market; hence, their retail prices are higher than the other agricultural products which were produced with the use of pesticides. In this context, Green house production is widely used all over the world to with minimal pesticides and weedicides including Sri Lanka. Automated Greenhouses can be used to increase the production with a minimum amount of human labor. With less use of human hours, it will produce more harvest than conventional Greenhouses which need constant human attention and care. The installation of automated Greenhouses is costly although their long-term benefits are higher than a conventional one. For this reason, introducing the concept to cultivators would be difficult, as they are reluctant to invest their money on an unfamiliar technology. There is a hesitance to embrace the technology since they don’t have first-hand experience in operating an automated Greenhouse. Therefore, in this paper we present a simulated model of automated Greenhouse using Augmented reality, through which a client can visually experience the workings of IoT Greenhouse based on theoretical models beforehand to make an informed decision to invest in automated Greenhouses.

Introduction

Greenhouses form an integral part of agriculture. They are used to grow specific plants under specific controlled conditions for optimum results. They allow farmers to grow plants and yield a better production throughout the year regardless of climatic conditions. In today’s world, greenhouses contribute to farming a lot due to global warming and various climatic changes. The next evolution of the greenhouse concept is it being automated with the help of Internet of Things (IOT). Automated greenhouses are higher in cost, but more efficient in precision farming than conventional methods. Greenhouse automation and control work in a similar way as industrial applications. Several calibrated environmental sensors are used to control instrumentation devices to restrict climatic conditions. Each sensor and it’s response should be analyzed separately for better results Currently in Sri Lanka, many organizations/companies are in the process of automating the greenhouses using IOT to yield maximum output in order to improve the agricultural activities and maintenance. Though several technological methods are used to improve the productivity it isn’t that effective or adequate to yield the maximum output since there’s a gap in agriculturalists’ understanding and their using the technological aspects. As a result, most of the agriculturalists are reluctant to use the advanced products and also the technological companies are finding it really difficult to communicate/explain the final look and convince them the advantages of using automated greenhouses. The lack of first-hand experience of an automated greenhouse and understanding of technological enhancement or technological usage in improving the greenhouse are some of the major issues/barriers in marketing automated greenhouse solutions to several agriculturalists as well as the local farmers/ cultivators at ground level. Our solution is a simulation of an IoT based greenhouse named AR-Glasshouse that would help make the IoT vendors clearly communicate the need for an automated greenhouse solution to farmers through augmented reality visualization. This application consists of 4 major components which are mathematically modelled, built and simulated on 4 major greenhouse governing factors.

Background and Literature Survey

Several researches are being held to aid farming activities, but they seem to be out of reach for many. Generally farming is done by a large group of people around the world. It is a problem of scalability faced by industry which has few experts and very large base of people in their expert service needs. Technology can play a major role here where AR can be used. Augmented reality (AR) technology has advanced in the past few years with a growing number of low-cost AR devices becoming available to the general public. AR techniques have demonstrated the capacity to optimize task efficiency in a broad range of industries and provide engaging entertainment and education experiences. One of the extremely underexplored areas is its application in broad agriculture sector”. There aren’t much known products which incorporates both to provide a simulation feature on the domain of agriculture. Results of research using mathematical models in systems by numerical and analytical methods play an important role in implementation of the projects. A mathematically modelled IoT based greenhouse simulation solution is not as accessible and easily available as a mass market solution that can be used by IoT vendors to communicate their ideas efficiently. There are a limited number of studies that are conducted to analyze the distribution of environmental factors in Greenhouses. Some of the available studies that analyzed distributions are,

  • A. Temperature distribution study for Malaysia Broiler house (research project conducted by universiti Teknologi, Malaysia)

This research was done to find out the temperature distribution of heat sources for a broiler house in Malaysia, based on the energy consumption of the bulbs. Four types of bulbs have been used to illustrate the heat sources. Performance of the bulbs is compared in analyzing the results of the research. Temperature distribution of a phoenix 120w bulb and a fluorescent bulb is plotted against specified time durations. As compared to other bulbs, researchers have found that the phoenix 120w incandescent bulb provides the highest localized heat.

  • B. Impact of Direct Lightning Luminaries’ Luminous Intensity Distribution on Lighting Quality in interiors (Research project from Warsaw University of Technology, Poland)

This research has been conducted regarding lighting in physical environments, balancing necessary illumination without causing a discomforting glare. Researchers have considered different physical environments in office and calculated UGR (Unified Glare Ratings) values for each location and compared that with recommended UGR values. The aim of this research is to assess the impact of luminaries’ luminous intensity distribution on selected qualitative metrics of interior luminous environment. They have used 4 types of Luminaries of two wide shapes and two narrow shapes and used them with five rooms with different length, width and height combinations. Luminaries layout were changed in each room. UGR values were calculated for each situation and compared with five different rooms for each Luminaire type. They have come to the conclusion that narrow Luminous intensity distribution enables limiting UGR value to 19 and maintenance of the same despite of room sizes, while wider LIDs increase UGR values.

Methodology

Mathematical modelling is describing a system or a component using mathematical concepts. It is used to translate problems of an application to mathematical formulations. Majority of the application development is based on mathematical modelling of factors which control the greenhouse state such as soil conditions, humidity, temperature, light intensity to realistically model and visualize the green house in augmented reality.
